Kilotest

Kilotest performs a thousand tests on a web page.

A thousand? Really?

Ensemble testing

Kilotest does ensemble testing, with a focus on accessibility.

Software fails unless tested. Efficient testing usually leverages automated tools and corrects the defects that they discover, then employs human testers.

There are numerous automated testing tools. They vary in what and how they test. Each tool may discover only a fraction of the issues that multiple tools discover.

Ensemble testing takes advantage of this complementarity. We test with several tools, then standardize and integrate their results.

The tools

Kilotest leverages an ensemble of 11 tools developed by teams around the world, including the USA, Canada, Denmark, Australia, and Portugal. Some of the tools are under active development, and when they get updated Kilotest users get the benefits.

The tools perform more than a thousand tests in total. Open-source software developed initially at CVS Health drives the tools and distills their findings into about 300 issues.

The tools in the ensemble are independent of each other and of Kilotest, so Kilotest does not guarantee that every reported issue will be a problem for you. If you want such a guarantee, you might prefer a single tool, such as Axe-core, that is less comprehensive but claims to avoid false positives.

Status

Kilotest is a proof of concept, launched in November 2025. Your suggestions, bug reports, and collaboration proposals are welcome! Feel free to post them on GitHub or email them to info@kilotest.com.

In the current version, users can get results of already performed tests and can recommend new tests. Kilotest managers can approve recommendations.
